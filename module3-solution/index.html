Joffrey's Last Meal

The first dish is a very creamy soup of wild mushrooms and buttered snails, served in gilded bowls. The second course is a pastry coffyn filled with pork, pine nuts, and eggs. Then you can sample sweetcorn fritters and hot oatbread baked with bits of date, apple, and orange, as well as the rib of a wild boar. Stew Worth It All

The beer is brown, the bread black, the stew is creamy white. Is is served it in a trencher hollowed out of a stale loaf. It was thick with leeks, orange carrots, barley, pink beetroots and turnips white and yellow, along with clams and chunks of cod and crabmeat, swimming in a stock of heavy cream and butter.
